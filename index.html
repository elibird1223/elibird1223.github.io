What Can You Do with an Economics Degree?

Economics offers versatile career opportunities, reflected in the diverse electives within the major. The Department has grouped electives into Econ Tracks, each aligning with specific career paths. Though these tracks don't appear on transcripts or diplomas, they can help guide your graduation plan.

The tracks are grouped based on similar skills that you develop in the econ major that are used within those careers. Students are welcome to take any elective, even if it spans multiple tracks.

💰

Management/Finance

The Management/Finance Track explores market competition, strategic decision making, and regulation. These analytic skills are readily applied in many business and finance roles.

Related Careers

  • Actuary
  • Business Analyst
  • Economic Consultant
  • Financial Advisor
  • Financial Analyst
  • Investment Research Analyst
  • Management Consultant
  • Manager
  • Market Research Analyst

Related Econ Electives

  • ECON 420 – Antitrust & Regulation
  • ECON 442 – Behavioral Economics
  • ECON 450 – Financial Economics
  • ECON 453 – Monetary Theory
  • ECON 458 – International Trade
  • ECON 476 – Industrial Organization
  • ECON 478 – Game Theory

Complements

  • Minors: Foreign language; Strategy; Business; Entrepreneurship
  • Non‑Econ Courses: IS 110 (Spreadsheets); IS 201 (Management Information Systems)
  • BYU Clubs: Management Consulting Association
  • Graduate Programs: MBA
💻

Data Analytics

The Data Analytics Track enhances the data analysis core curriculum with additional tools used to investigate large amounts of data. This prepares students for cutting-edge jobs in tech companies and supports the most quantitative roles in finance or academics.

Related Careers

  • Actuary
  • Data Analyst
  • Data Scientist
  • Financial Analyst
  • Investment Research Analyst
  • Market Research Analyst
  • Statistician (Economic)

Related Econ Electives

  • ECON 442 – Behavioral Economics
  • ECON 484 – Machine Learning
  • ECON 588 – Advanced Econometrics

Complements

  • Majors: ACME
  • Minors: Computer Science; Statistics
  • Non‑Econ Courses: CS 111 (Python); LING 360 (Text Processing); STAT 250 (R Programming); STAT 274 (Theory of Interest); STAT 281 (Data Visualization)
  • Graduate Programs: Masters in Data Analytics; PhD
  • Software: Dash; Shiny; Streamlit
🏛️

Policy

The Policy Track applies economic analysis across a variety of fields where public policy is at the forefront. These courses build applied econometrics and policy skills useful in government, research, and policy careers.

Related Careers

  • Demographer
  • Foreign Service Officer
  • Journalist
  • Policy Analyst
  • Think Tank Researcher

Related Econ Electives

  • ECON 440 – Environmental Economics
  • ECON 442 – Behavioral Economics
  • ECON 461 – Education Economics
  • ECON 463 – Labor Economics
  • ECON 465 – Health Economics
  • ECON 468 – Urban Economics
  • ECON 475 – Public Economics

Complements

  • Minors: Healthcare Leadership; Political Science; Urban & Regional Planning (Geography)
  • BYU Clubs: Foreign Service Student Organization; Model UN
  • Graduate Programs: MD; Masters of Public Policy
🌍

Global Issues

The Global Issues Track emphasizes macroeconomics across countries, including measurement challenges and international policy. This toolkit is especially useful for internationally oriented careers.

Related Careers

  • Demographer
  • Foreign Service Officer
  • Intelligence Officer (CIA)
  • Journalist
  • Policy Analyst
  • Think Tank Researcher

Related Econ Electives

  • ECON 431 – Economic Development
  • ECON 432 – Economic Growth
  • ECON 440 – Environmental Economics
  • ECON 458 – International Trade
  • ECON 459 – International Monetary Economics

Complements

  • Minors: Area Studies; Foreign Language; Global and Community Impact; Global Business & Literacy; Global Women’s Studies
  • BYU Clubs: Ballard Center Social Impact Association; Model UN; Students for International Development
  • BYU Programs: International Studies Programs
  • Graduate Programs: Masters of Public Policy
🎓

Pre-PhD

The Pre‑PhD Track cultivates deeper analytic and empirical skills needed to pursue rigorous, independent research. The 500‑level courses expose students to the first‑year curriculum in most economics, finance, or accounting PhD programs.

Related Careers

  • Data Scientist
  • Federal Reserve Research Assistant
  • Professor
  • Think Tank Researcher

Related Econ Electives

  • ECON 580 – Advanced Micro Theory 1
  • ECON 581 – Advanced Macroeconomics
  • ECON 582 – Advanced Micro Theory 2
  • ECON 588 – Advanced Econometrics

Complements

  • Majors: Mathematics
  • Minors: Mathematics
  • Non‑Econ Courses: Data analytics courses
  • Graduate Programs: PhD (Economics, Finance, Accounting, or related disciplines)
  • Software: Mathematica; MATLAB; Stata (including Stata programming workshops)
⚖️

Law

The Law Track focuses on reasoning skills and policy issues that are most relevant to service in government. It provides an excellent basis for graduate study in law or work in advocacy and government.

Related Careers

  • Federal Reserve Research Assistant
  • Foreign Service Officer
  • Intelligence Officer (CIA)
  • Policy Analyst

Related Econ Electives

  • ECON 420 – Antitrust & Regulation
  • ECON 421 – Law & Economics
  • ECON 463 – Labor Economics
  • ECON 477 – Political Economics
  • ECON 478 – Game Theory

Complements

  • Minors: Philosophy; Logic; Political Science
  • Non‑Econ Courses: HIST 392 (US Constitution); PHIL 205 (Logic); POLI 201 & 202 (Western Political Heritage); STDEV 168R; STDEV 365
  • BYU Clubs: Civic Engagement Leadership; Pre‑Law Student Association
  • Graduate Programs: Juris Doctor (JD)
